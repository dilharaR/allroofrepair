Roofing FAQ

Roof restoration is the process of repairing and renewing a roof to extend its lifespan and improve its appearance. The process typically involves cleaning, repairing, and sealing the roof.

Here are the steps involved in roof restoration:

  • Inspection: The first step is to have a qualified roofing contractor inspect your roof. The contractor will look for any damage, leaks, or other problems.

  • Cleaning: Once the roof has been inspected, it will need to be cleaned. This can be done by pressure washing.

  • Repairs: Any damage to the roof will need to be repaired. This may involve replacing tiles, shingles, or flashing.

  • Sealing: Once the repairs have been made, the roof will need to be sealed. This will help to protect the roof from water damage and extend its lifespan.

Roof restoration can be a costly project, but it can be a wise investment. A well-maintained roof can protect your home from the elements and save you money on energy bills.


Here are some of the benefits of roof restoration:

  • Extends the life of your roof: A well-maintained roof can last for many years.

  • Improves the energy efficiency of your home: A properly sealed roof can help to keep your home cooler in the summer and warmer in the winter.

  • Prevents leaks: A leaky roof can cause damage to your home and belongings.

  • Improves the appearance of your home: A new roof can make your home look its best.

If you are considering roof restoration, it is important to get quotes from several different contractors. You should also ask about the type of materials that will be used and the warranty that is offered.


Here are some tips for choosing a roof restoration contractor:

  • Get multiple estimates.

  • Ask about the contractor’s experience and qualifications.

  • Check references

  • Make sure the contractor is licensed and insured.

  • Get everything in writing, including the scope of work, the materials that will be used, and the warranty.

The cost of restoring a roof depends on a variety of factors, including the size and type of roof, the extent of damage, and the materials used. In general, you can expect to pay between $3,000 and $10,000 for a roof restoration.

Here are some of the factors that can affect the cost of roof restoration:

  • Size of the roof: The larger the roof, the more expensive it will be to restore.

  • Type of roof: The type of roof material will also affect the cost of restoration. For example, metal roofs are typically more expensive to restore than asphalt shingles.

What to do if roof is leaking during storm?

If your roof is leaking during a storm, it is important to take action immediately to prevent further damage. Here are some steps you can take:

  • Locate the source of the leak. The first step is to find where the water is coming from. This may be obvious if you can see a hole in the roof, but if not, you may need to use a flashlight to look for signs of water damage, such as wet insulation or drywall.

  • Contain the water. Once you have located the source of the leak, you need to contain the water to prevent it from spreading. You can do this by placing buckets or towels under the leak. If the leak is large, you may need to use a tarp to cover the roof.

  • Call a roofer.Once you have contained the water, you need to call a roofer to repair the leak. Do not attempt to repair the leak yourself, as this could make the problem worse.

  • Document the damage.Once the leak has been repaired, you should document the damage. This will be helpful if you need to file an insurance claim.

Here are some additional tips for dealing with a leaking roof:

  • Do not climb on the roof - It is dangerous to climb on a roof during a storm. If you need to access the roof, wait until the storm has passed.

  • Do not use electrical appliances - If water has gotten into electrical appliances, do not use them until they have been inspected and repaired by a qualified electrician.

  • Be aware of mold - Mold can grow in wet areas, so it is important to dry the area around the leak as soon as possible. If you see any signs of mold, such as black spots or a musty odor, you should call a professional to remove it.

By following these steps, you can minimize the damage caused by a leaking roof.


What is the main cause of roof leaks?

There are many reasons why a roof might leak, but the most common causes include:

  • Age: As roofs age, they become more susceptible to leaks. The materials that make up a roof, such as shingles, tiles, or metal, can deteriorate over time due to exposure to the elements.

  • Weather: Heavy rain, hail, or snow can put a lot of stress on a roof and cause it to leak.

  • Debris: Debris, such as leaves, branches, or even toys, can collect on a roof and block gutters or downspouts, which can lead to leaks.

  • Improper installation: If a roof is not installed properly, it is more likely to leak. This can happen if the materials are not properly aligned or if the wrong type of material is used for the climate.

  • Damage from animals: Animals, such as squirrels or raccoons, can sometimes damage roofs by chewing on shingles or tiles. This can lead to leaks.

  • Wind damage: Strong winds can tear shingles off a roof or damage flashing, which can lead to leaks.

  • Ice dams: Ice dams can form on roofs during the winter when snow melts and then refreezes. This can cause water to back up and leak into the home.

If you notice any signs of a leak, such as water stains on the ceiling or walls, it is important to have the roof inspected by a qualified roofer as soon as possible. Leaks can cause extensive damage to your home, so it is important to get them repaired as soon as possible.

Here are some tips for preventing roof leaks:

  • Have your roof inspected regularly: A qualified roofer can inspect your roof for any signs of wear and tear and recommend repairs or replacement if necessary.

  • Clean your gutters and downspouts regularly:Gutters and downspouts help to prevent water from pooling on your roof, which can lead to leaks.

  • Remove debris from your roof: Debris can block gutters and downspouts and put stress on your roof.

  • Secure loose shingles or tiles: Loose shingles or tiles can be a sign of a problem. Secure them as soon as possible.

  • Repair any damage to your roof: If you notice any damage to your roof, such as missing shingles or tiles, have it repaired as soon as possible.

  • Consider a metal roof: Metal roofs are more durable than other types of roofs and are less likely to leak.

By following these tips, you can help to prevent roof leaks and protect your home from damage.
